About Us

The Fight Against Neurodegenerative Disease

One in ten people over the age of 65 have Alzheimer’s Disease and other forms of dementia. That comes out to 44 million people in the world who are afflicted. By 2050, healthcare costs for dementia in the US alone could rise to $1.1 trillion, over 325% more than what they are now. In a future where the average lifespan increases, risks for neurodegenerative disease will only go up.

But there are ways we can help. Early detection of Alzheimer’s, Parkinson’s, Huntington’s, and other neurodegenerative diseases may greatly assist in patient care. At Cognitive, we aim to use the results of our Neurocognitive Screening test to better evaluate cognitive function through simple tasks representative of cognitive health.

Putting your health in your hands

Patient care can be confusing even for healthcare professionals, so why should it be expected to be any different for patients? A 2009 study showed that the language that doctors and patients used was radically different, even stating that many patients did not truly understand the language involved in their treatment. With the increasing rate of medical innovation, the gap between patients and doctors is ever-important. This gap presents the need for patient-supplements that allow for patients to evaluate themselves and understand their own health.

Cognitive is one of the first innovators in a movement that seeks to radically transform healthcare by returning patient care back to the hands of the patient. Our goals as a company are to promote self-care through our app, the first neurocognitive diagnosis tool available on mobile devices. We believe that our commitment to self-care will help in the early diagnosis of millions of patients with cognitive impairment in the future.

Disclaimer

The Cognitive Neurocognitive Screening Diagnosis Tool is a patient supplement that allows patients to evaluate their cognitive abilities through simple tasks. While the test provides a metric to evaluate cognitive function, it cannot determine whether a patient has a disease like Alzheimer’s, for example. The test is not liable for any false negatives/positives that are presented through results.
